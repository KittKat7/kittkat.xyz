I Lost the Game

The Game, oh how elusive,
An endless loop of thought intrusive.
It creeps in when you least expect,
And once you're caught, you can't neglect.

The rules are simple, yet profound,
Don't think of it, don't make a sound.
For once you do, you must admit,
You've lost the game, you can't outwit.

It's played by many, far and wide,
A mental game that's hard to hide.
It brings a smile, a laugh, a sigh,
And unites us, no matter why.

So let us play, and play it well,
A game that words alone can't tell.
For though we'll lose, we'll all agree,
The Game's the best of company.
